Memory Matters

Services

When providing assistance to patients with Alzheimer's disease, it's essential to offer a range of services that address their unique needs and challenges. The goal is to enhance their quality of life, promote independence, and provide support for both the individuals affected by Alzheimer's and their caregivers.

Support

  • Support groups for both individuals with Alzheimer's and their caregivers.
  • Opportunities to share experiences, coping strategies, and emotional support.
  • Educational resources to help caregivers understand and manage the challenges associated with Alzheimer's.

Purpose:

To create a community for patients and caregivers facing Alzheimer's, offering a platform to share experiences, coping strategies, and emotional support.

Activities:

Regular meetings, facilitated discussions, and educational sessions on Alzheimer's management and caregiving.

Benefits:

Emotional support, reduced isolation, and the exchange of practical advice.

Fundraise

Purpose:

To raise funds for Alzheimer's research, support programs, and services.

Activities:

Events such as charity walks, runs, galas, and online crowdfunding campaigns.

Benefits:

Financial support for research, awareness campaigns, and assistance programs for affected individuals and their families.

Volunteer

Purpose:

To provide hands-on assistance and companionship to individuals living with Alzheimer's and their caregivers.

Activities:

Visiting patients, assisting with daily tasks, providing respite care, and organizing community events.

Benefits:

Direct support for those affected, relief for caregivers, and increased community engagement.

*High schoolers can earn community service hours through Memory Matters*
